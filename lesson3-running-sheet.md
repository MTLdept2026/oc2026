# Lesson 3 Running Sheet

## Lesson Snapshot

- Topic: Wawancara Bersama Tetamu Misteri
- Class: 1G2
- Duration: 2 periods, about 50 minutes
- Mystery guests available: Hang Nadim, Mahsuri, Mat Jenin
- Open classroom focus: Students transfer questioning, evidence, and reflection skills to a new mystery guest and finish with a personal character takeaway.

## Learning Outcomes

- Students ask purposeful interview questions in Malay using familiar question words.
- Students gather clues from AI responses to infer a mystery guest's story, actions, and likely identity.
- Students identify one key value or lesson from the guest's story and support it with evidence.
- Students reflect on their favourite character and explain how they will use that lesson in future challenges.

## Success Criteria

- I can ask at least 3 good questions and 1 follow-up.
- I can explain what happened in my guest's story and why the guest acted that way.
- I can identify one important value or lesson with evidence.
- I can write a reflection about what I learned and how I will use it later.

## Materials

- `wartawan-cilik-lesson3.html`
- Student devices
- Projector
- Optional copies of LK2 and LK3 for quick recall

## Grouping

- Best grouping: pairs or triads
- Suggested roles:
- Penanya
- Pencatat
- Penyemak bukti

## 50-Minute Flow

### 0-5 min: Launch

- Teacher script:
- "Hari ini kamu akan masuk studio temu duga untuk menyiasat seorang tetamu misteri."
- "Kamu belum tahu namanya. Kamu perlu cari cerita, tindakan, sebab, dan pengajarannya."
- Student action:
- Open the site.
- Listen to the mission and look at the timing guide.
- Teacher checkpoint:
- Students understand that the goal is not just to guess the name quickly.

### 5-10 min: Recall Ringkas

- Teacher script:
- "Sebelum jumpa tetamu misteri, aktifkan semula otak wartawan kamu."
- "Ingat satu kata tanya, satu nilai atau pengajaran, dan satu ayat bukti daripada pelajaran lepas."
- Student action:
- Complete the short recall section.
- Share one answer with a partner.
- Teacher checkpoint:
- Students can still retrieve old vocabulary and questioning structures.

### 10-15 min: Choose A Mystery Dossier

- Teacher script:
- "Setiap kumpulan pilih satu fail misteri."
- "Jangan buru nama. Cari dahulu apa yang berlaku dan mengapa."
- Student action:
- Choose one of three dossiers.
- Read the short clue card.
- Make a first prediction about story and lesson.
- Teacher checkpoint:
- Every group has selected one dossier and one possible value or lesson.

### 15-22 min: Forge Questions

- Teacher script:
- "Soalan yang baik datang daripada petunjuk, bukan tekaan rawak."
- "Tanya tentang cerita, keputusan, dan sebab, baru identiti."
- Student action:
- Draft one main question and one follow-up.
- Use prompt stems if needed.
- Teacher checkpoint:
- Before groups move to chat, check:
- one clear main question
- one follow-up
- one clue note

### 22-35 min: Interview Studio

- Teacher script:
- "Sekarang masuk studio. Dengar baik-baik. Catat fakta penting."
- "Kalau tetamu tidak beri nama, itu memang sebahagian daripada tugas kamu."
- Student action:
- Interview the AI guest.
- Ask 3 questions and at least 1 follow-up.
- Record story, motivation, and lesson.
- Teacher checkpoint:
- Students are asking beyond "Siapa kamu?"
- Students capture one useful evidence point from the answers.

### 35-43 min: Kad Profil Rahsia

- Teacher script:
- "Sekarang kita bina profil tanpa mendedahkan identiti terlalu cepat."
- "Sekarang kamu boleh tulis nama sebenar watak yang kamu temu duga."
- "Pilih dua nilai RECIPE yang paling jelas dan beri bukti untuk setiap nilai."
- Student action:
- Complete the profile card after identifying the mystery guest.
- Fill in:
- the mystery guest's name
- what happened
- what the guest did
- two RECIPE values
- evidence for each RECIPE value
- Teacher checkpoint:
- Students can explain the guest's actions and justify each chosen RECIPE value with evidence.

### 43-50 min: Reflection and Closing

- Teacher script:
- "Bahagian paling penting sekarang ialah apa yang kamu pelajari."
- "Pilih sekurang-kurangnya satu situasi yang mungkin berlaku dalam hidup kamu."
- "Fikirkan: jika watak ini berada dalam situasi itu, apa yang akan dia buat, dan mengapa?"
- "Sekarang pilih dua nilai RECIPE daripada watak itu dan jelaskan bagaimana kamu akan cuba tunjukkan kedua-dua nilai itu pada masa depan."
- Student action:
- Complete final reflection:
- my favourite character
- tick at least one current-life scenario
- predict how the character would act in that situation and why
- two RECIPE values I want to practise
- how I will show each value in future situations
- Share one reflection if time permits.
- Teacher checkpoint:
- Students end with transfer to life, not only identity recall.

## Differentiated Supports

- Support:
- allow students to use prompt buttons first
- permit oral rehearsal before typing
- let them focus on story and motivation before value naming

- Core:
- require 3 questions, 1 follow-up, and 1 evidence-based lesson

- Stretch:
- require students to explain why their chosen lesson is stronger than another possible lesson
- ask students to compare the mystery guest with a known character from earlier lessons

## Notes On The Three Mystery Guests

- Hang Nadim:
- rich for courage, quick thinking, care for others, wise action

- Mahsuri:
- rich for honesty, dignity, resilience, and responding to injustice

- Mat Jenin:
- rich for dreams, motivation, mistakes, and the lesson that hope must be joined with action and focus

## Non-Negotiables

- Students should not get the name too early.
- Students should ask about events, motivations, and choices before identity.
- The written product should stay short and manageable.
- The final reflection and scenario prediction are the main learning endpoints.
